# Scouting Brand Guide for AI Tools

A self-contained brand reference for generating Scouting America themed
materials: flyers, web pages, slide decks, email, social graphics, signage.
It requires nothing else from this repository. Paste this whole file into any
AI tool along with your task, for example:

> "I want to design a flyer for a treasure hunt that we're doing. Here are the
> details. Follow the attached brand guide."

Everything here is distilled from the Scouting America 2024 Brand Guidelines
plus contrast and print verification done while building a design system on
top of them. Where a rule protects accessibility or trademark compliance it is
marked MUST. Everything else is strong guidance.

---

## 1. The brand family

One parent brand, four programs. Pick the program that matches your audience
before making any design decision; almost every choice below varies by program.

| Program | Audience | Identity in one line |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Scouting America (parent) | Everyone | Civic, trustworthy, heritage. Red, white, and blue. |
| Cub Scouts | Kids K-5 and their parents | Warm, playful, kid-bold. Blue and gold, like the uniform. |
| Scouts BSA | Ages 11-17 | Earnest, capable, traditional. Olive, tan, field-guide calm. |
| Venturing | Ages 14-20, co-ed | Bold, peer-led, expedition sharp. Green and trail-marker yellow. |
| Sea Scouts | Ages 14-20, co-ed | Editorial nautical, mature, drafted. Navy and signal yellow. |

Official taglines (use verbatim, never embellish):

- Cub Scouts: "Do Your Best. Have Fun Doing It."
- Scouts BSA: "Be Prepared."
- Venturing: "Challenge Yourself. Challenge Your World."
- Sea Scouts: "Live the Adventure. Navigate the World."

### Contrast rules (MUST)

- Body text needs 4.5:1 contrast against its background, large text (18px+
  bold or 24px+ regular) needs 3:1. The ink and secondary-text values above
  are pre-verified against their program surfaces.
- The gold rule: dark text on any of the golds/yellows (#FDC116, #FCD116,
  #FFCC00) only passes at 16px and larger. Never set small text on a gold
  fill. On a gold button or banner, use the program's dark blue or green ink
  and keep the type big.
- Never use white text on gold or yellow at any size.
- Never fake hierarchy by lowering text opacity below roughly 80%; pick a real
  mid-tone (like #46587A on Cub surfaces) instead.

color. Reversed reproduction (on a dark background): white only. Never tint

## 3. Typography

Two families across every program. The differentiation lives in how the
display face is set, not in swapping fonts.

- **Display / headlines**: Montserrat (SIL Open Font License, free).
  Substitute any geometric sans (Futura, Proxima Nova, Arial in a pinch) if
  Montserrat is unavailable.
- **Body**: Source Serif 4 (also OFL). Substitute a readable transitional
  serif (Georgia, Charter). Body text in a serif is part of the brand's
  warm, printed-handbook feel; do not set long body copy in the sans.

Per-program display treatment:

| Program | Weight | Case | Tracking | Style |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Parent | 700 | Sentence case | normal | upright |
| Cub Scouts | 900 (heaviest available) | Sentence case | slightly tight | upright |
| Scouts BSA | 700 | Sentence case | normal | upright |
| Venturing | 800 | ALL CAPS | wide (+8%) | upright |
| Sea Scouts | 600 | Sentence case | slightly tight | italic |

A Cub Scouts headline should look like it was stamped by an enthusiastic kid
with a fat marker. A Sea Scouts headline should look like the masthead of a
sailing journal. If your output could swap programs without anyone noticing,
the type is not working hard enough.

## 4. Shape, texture, and per-program motifs

Corner rounding, rule weight, and shadow all carry personality:

| Program | Corner radius | Rule/keyline weight | Shadow character |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Cub Scouts | generous (~14px) | chunky (3px) | soft, blue-tinted, toy-like |
| Scouts BSA | crisp (~4px) | traditional (2px) | flat, matte, archival |
| Venturing | near-sharp (~2px) | blocky (2px) | strong and directional |
| Sea Scouts | hairline (~1px) | drafted (1px) | almost none |

Each program owns a decorative motif. Use it as a horizontal divider, a strip
along the top of a featured panel, or a border on printed pieces. Draw it
simply; these are describable to any image or layout tool:

- **Cub Scouts**: a row of small gold diamonds (rotated squares) with even
  gaps, echoing the paw-print badge rhythm.
- **Scouts BSA**: a plain double rule, one thicker line over one thin, in tan.
- **Venturing**: a row of repeating chevrons (^ ^ ^) in green, pointing up
  like a mountain ridgeline.
- **Sea Scouts**: a single continuous wave line in navy, drawn thin.

Watermarks: a large, single-color, very low opacity (6-10%) emblem or motif
adds depth. Use one color only, never the full-color mark as a watermark.
Bleeding off a corner is allowed ONLY for the motifs above or your own
artwork; an official mark used as a watermark must appear whole, uncropped,
and with clear space, because the license forbids partial reproduction
(see section 8).

## 5. Layout principles

Learned the hard way; these keep output from looking machine-generated:

- Emphasis comes from tone and weight, not from colored edge bars. To make a
  panel important: warmer surface, a full hairline outline, a heavier
  headline, or the program motif along its top. Never a colored stripe down
  one side.
- Section headers work as editorial kickers: a small uppercase label with wide
  tracking sitting on a full-width keyline, optionally numbered ("01
  Schedule"). Not a colored pill or badge floating above a heading.
- Break symmetry on purpose. If you have three content blocks, make one
  dominant. Equal thirds with matching icon-title-blurb structure reads as
  template output.
- Let the paper show. These palettes are built on warm paper surfaces;
  generous margins and unfilled space are on-brand. Resist edge-to-edge color.
- Real details beat abstractions. "Saturday, March 14, 9 AM, Riverside Park
  pavilion" belongs in big type. Vague benefit statements do not.

## 6. Voice and copy

General rules for all programs:

- Write concretely. Name the activity, the place, the time, what to bring.
- Use real scouting vocabulary correctly: Cub Scouts belong to dens and packs;
  Scouts BSA to patrols and troops; Venturers to crews; Sea Scouts to ships.
  Adults are leaders or volunteers. Advancement terms: Bobcat through Arrow of
  Light (Cub), Eagle Scout (Scouts BSA), quarterdeck roles like boatswain
  (Sea Scouts).
- Never use em-dashes. Use commas, colons, periods, or parentheses.
- Ban the filler CTA. "Learn More" says nothing; "See what packs do" or
  "Meet the crews" says something.
- Do not invent or extend official taglines and mottos. "Be Prepared." is
  complete.

Per-program voice:

| Program | Speak to | Sound like | Sample CTA verbs |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Cub Scouts | Parents first, kids over their shoulder | Warm, energetic, plain words, short sentences, reading level ~grade 4 | Join, Build, Race, Explore, Camp |
| Scouts BSA | The scout directly | Earnest, capable, respectful of their independence | Lead, Earn, Trek, Serve |
| Venturing | Young adults as peers | Direct, motivational, zero condescension | Plan, Climb, Paddle, Decide |
| Sea Scouts | Young adults, slightly formal | Nautical, precise, a little romantic about the water | Sail, Navigate, Crew, Take the helm |

## 7. Design tells to avoid (MUST read)

These patterns make output look AI-generated regardless of colors and fonts.
Avoid all of them:

- A 2-4px colored bar along the left edge of a card, quote, or list item.
- Icons trapped in small tinted rounded squares.
- Colored pill badges floating above headings as section labels.
- Three perfectly parallel feature cards, each icon + two-word title + blurb.
- Gradient hero backgrounds with blurred glowing blobs.
- Emoji used as icons or bullet points.
- Drop shadows, bevels, glows, or outlines applied to any official mark
  (also a trademark violation, see below).
- Sparkle imagery, generic mountain-and-flag clip art, rocket ships.
- Body copy patterns: "isn't just X, it's Y", "Whether you're A or B",
  rule-of-three adjective chains, em-dashes, exclamation marks in pairs.

## 8. Trademarks and legal (MUST)

- The fleur-de-lis, program logos, "Be Prepared," and program names are
  federally protected trademarks (36 U.S.C. 27, plus USPTO registrations).
- Only registered volunteers and council/national employees may use official
  marks, only to promote approved scouting programs and activities, in
  channels their leadership has approved.
- Never recolor, crop, add effects to, tint, screen, or redraw an official
  mark. One-color versions may print in black or any single dark color;
  reversed versions must be white; that is the entire flexibility you have.
- Never place official marks alongside the branding of another youth-serving
  organization (4-H, Girl Scouts, YMCA, Boys & Girls Clubs).
- If you are not authorized, or your tool cannot verify the mark files are
  licensed, design without logos: the palettes, typography, motifs, and voice
  above carry the identity on their own. A gold diamond strip on Cub blue
  says "Cub Scouts" without touching a trademark.

## 9. Worked example: Cub Scout treasure hunt flyer

Request: "I want to design a flyer for a treasure hunt that we're doing.
Here are the details: Pack 42, Saturday April 18, 10 AM, Riverside Park,
bring a compass, snacks provided."

Applying this guide:

1. **Program**: kids K-5, so Cub Scouts. Blue #003F87, gold #FDC116, warm
   paper surface #FFFCF4, ink #0A1A33.
2. **Headline**: Montserrat 900 (or heaviest available geometric sans),
   sentence case, big: "The Pack 42 treasure hunt." A concrete subhead in the
   serif: "Maps, compasses, and one hidden chest somewhere in Riverside Park."
3. **Layout**: paper-colored background with wide margins. A row of gold
   diamonds under the headline as the divider. One dominant panel for the
   details (day, time, place, what to bring) set large; do not bury logistics
   in small print, parents skim.
4. **Emphasis**: the details panel gets a thin blue outline and a slightly
   warmer gold-wash background (#FFF4D1). No left stripe, no icon chips.
5. **Accent**: one gold call-to-action band near the bottom: "Every scout
   walks away with treasure." Dark blue text, large, because small dark text
   on gold fails contrast.
6. **Voice**: short warm sentences. "Bring your compass and your den. We
   provide the map, the snacks, and the treasure." Reading level around
   grade 4, no em-dashes, no "Join us for an unforgettable adventure!"
7. **Marks**: if the pack has licensed logo files, the Cub Scouts logo goes
   in a corner at full color on the light background, unmodified, with clear
   space around it. If not, the diamond motif and palette do the branding.
8. **Print check**: blue is PMS 294 (CMYK 100, 48, 0, 44), gold is PMS 116
   (CMYK 0, 10, 100, 0). In grayscale the design still works because
   hierarchy comes from type weight and the outlined panel, not from color.

## 10. Pre-flight checklist

- [ ] One program chosen; palette, type treatment, and voice all match it
- [ ] All body text passes 4.5:1 contrast; nothing small sits on gold
- [ ] Display face is doing its program-specific work (weight, case, tracking)
- [ ] Emphasis via tone, outline, or motif; no colored edge bars anywhere
- [ ] No icon squircles, pill labels, emoji icons, or gradient blobs
- [ ] Copy is concrete, correctly uses den/pack/patrol/troop/crew/ship
- [ ] No em-dashes; no invented taglines; no filler CTAs
- [ ] Official marks (if any) are licensed, unmodified, with clear space
- [ ] Print pieces reference the Pantone/CMYK table and survive grayscale
